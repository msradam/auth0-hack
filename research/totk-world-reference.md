## 1. Major Events / Humanitarian Crises

### The Upheaval
The central catastrophe. When Link and Zelda discover a mummified Ganondorf beneath Hyrule Castle, his awakening triggers the Upheaval: the castle rises into the sky, ancient Zonai ruins surface, chasms (called "chasms" or "gulches") tear open across the landscape connecting to the Depths below, and a dark substance called Gloom spreads across the land. This is the inciting disaster for the entire humanitarian scenario.

### The Gloom
A toxic, corrosive dark substance that spreads from the Depths and from Ganondorf's influence. Contact with Gloom saps life force. Gloom-afflicted creatures and environments are a persistent hazard. Gloom pools appear in the overworld near chasms. For the demo: treat as a contamination/environmental hazard affecting populations.

### Regional Crises (each race has a specific crisis):

- **Zora's Domain — Sludge contamination**: A thick toxic sludge pollutes the waters around Zora's Domain, poisoning the water supply. King Dorephan is injured. The sludge rains down from a sky island where a corrupted spirit temple exists. Water — the lifeblood of the Zora — becomes unsafe.

- **Rito Village — Extreme blizzard**: An unnatural, perpetual blizzard engulfs the Hebra region and Rito Village. Temperatures become deadly. The Rito, being bird-people, are grounded. Food stores dwindle. Children and elderly are most vulnerable.

- **Goron City — Marbled Rock Roast addiction**: Gorons become addicted to Marbled Rock Roasts, a food item pushed by the Yiga Clan in disguise. Yunobo, the Goron leader, falls under the influence of a mind-controlling mask. The Goron economy is subverted.

- **Gerudo Town — Gibdo invasion and sandstorm**: A perpetual sandstorm blankets the Gerudo region. Gibdo (mummy-like monsters) swarm Gerudo Town in waves. The town's defenses are overwhelmed. Civilians shelter underground in the Gerudo Shelter. Riju, the Gerudo chief, leads the defense.

- **Lurelin Village — Pirate destruction**: Lurelin Village, a small fishing village on the southeast coast, is destroyed by monster pirates. Buildings are wrecked, treasure is looted, and villagers are displaced. This is the most direct "post-disaster reconstruction" scenario in the game.

- **Lookout Landing — Displaced persons hub**: After the Upheaval, Lookout Landing (near where the castle once stood) becomes the central refugee/coordination hub. Displaced Hylians gather here. It functions as the de facto emergency response headquarters.

- **Hateno Village — Political division**: Hateno Village faces an internal political crisis: a mayoral election between Reede (the incumbent, traditional farmer) and Cece (a fashion mogul pushing modernization). Not a disaster per se, but represents governance/community tension.

## 4. Major Settlements and Locations

### Central Hyrule
- **Lookout Landing** — Post-Upheaval emergency coordination hub. Has a general store, inn, cooking pot, Purah's research lab, Skyview Tower. The first place people gathered after the castle rose.
- **Hyrule Castle** — Floating above Central Hyrule, surrounded by Gloom. Inaccessible until endgame.
- **New Serenne Stable** — Near Lookout Landing; Impa camps here studying geoglyphs.
- **Riverside Stable**, **Wetland Stable** — Central Hyrule waypoints.

### Necluda Region
- **Hateno Village** — Large, thriving village. Has a dye shop, general store, inn, farms, school, and the Ancient Tech Lab on the hill above. Site of the mayoral election quest.
- **Kakariko Village** — Sheikah village. Paya is the new leader. Home to the Ring Ruins mystery.
- **Lurelin Village** — Coastal fishing village. Destroyed by pirates; rebuilding is a major side quest.
- **Dueling Peaks Stable**, **Lakeside Stable** — Necluda region stables.

### Lanayru Region
- **Zora's Domain** — Aquatic city built on waterfalls. Suffering sludge contamination. Has an inn, general store, blacksmith.
- **Mipha Court** — Memorial area in Zora's Domain.
- **Toto Lake** — Near Zora's Domain; site of historical research.

### Eldin Region
- **Goron City** — Built into the volcanic rock near Death Mountain. Has a general store, inn, and the YunoboCo HQ. Economy disrupted by Marbled Rock Roast scheme.
- **Eldin Canyon Stable**, **Foothill Stable** — Eldin area stables.

### Hebra Region
- **Rito Village** — Built on a tall rock pillar in Lake Totori. Engulfed in blizzard. Has a general store, inn. Food supply is critical.
- **Rito Stable**, **Snowfield Stable** — Hebra stables.
- **Tabantha Bridge Stable** — Gateway to Hebra.

### Gerudo Region
- **Gerudo Town** — Walled desert city. Under siege by Gibdos. Civilians sheltering in the Gerudo Shelter (underground).
- **Kara Kara Bazaar** — Oasis trading post on the approach to Gerudo Town.
- **Gerudo Canyon Stable** — Eastern approach to desert.

### Akkala Region
- **Tarrey Town** — Built by Hudson in BotW; now a full town on an island in Akkala. Home to Hudson Construction HQ, various shops, and a wedding chapel.
- **East Akkala Stable**, **South Akkala Stable** — Akkala stables.

### Great Hyrule Forest
- **Korok Forest** — Surrounded by the Lost Woods. Home of the Great Deku Tree and the Koroks. The Deku Tree is afflicted by Gloom in TotK.

### Faron Region
- **Lurelin Village** (also Necluda-adjacent)
- **Highland Stable**, **Lakeside Stable** — Faron region stables.

### Sky Islands
- **Great Sky Island** — Tutorial area; contains the Temple of Time ruins and Zonai constructs.
- **Various sky archipelagos** — Scattered above Hyrule; tied to each sage's temple.
- **Water Temple** (sky island above Lanayru), **Wind Temple** (sky above Hebra), **Fire Temple** (Death Mountain depths), **Lightning Temple** (Gerudo desert depths), **Spirit Temple** (Depths)

### The Depths
- Massive underground world beneath Hyrule, accessed through chasms. Pitch dark. Contains Lightroots (mirror Shrines above), Poes, Yiga Clan bases, mines, and the source of Gloom. Key locations include abandoned mines and Yiga Clan headquarters.

## 5. Side Quest Scenarios Mapped to Humanitarian Situations

|--------------|----------------------|
| **Lurelin Village Reconstruction** — Villagers need materials (wood, etc.) to rebuild homes destroyed by pirates. Rozel coordinates rebuilding. | Post-disaster shelter reconstruction; material aid distribution; community-led recovery |

| **Hateno Village election** — Reede vs. Cece mayoral race; community divided between tradition and modernization. | Local governance and democratic processes; community engagement |
| **Monster attacks on travelers** — Monsters terrorize roads between settlements. | Protection of civilians; safe passage/convoy needs |

| **Great Fairy restoration** — Great Fairies need the traveling musicians' troupe to reawaken them. | Restoration of essential services requiring coordination |
